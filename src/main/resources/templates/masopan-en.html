COWS
Rustic, independent and resilient, they have Slow Food status. The Rendena cow represents the history and tradition of this region. The first examples of the Rendena breed were introduced to the valley from Switzerland in 1700. With its dark coat and large, bright eyes, it is a dual-purpose cow (milk and meat). It is very well-suited to the summer pastures: trampling the soil and climbing steep slopes, it helps us to protect the land. The breed even has its own beauty contest, the “Giovenche di razza Rendena sfilata ... e dintorni” parade for Rendena heifers which is held in Pinzolo at the end of August to elect the best in show.


REGION
Sandwiched between the tonalite rock of the Adamello-Presanella ranges and the limestone of the Brenta Dolomites, the Val Rendena stretches from the hamlet of Verdesina up to Campo Carlo Magno, Madonna di Campiglio at a height of 1,650 m. It is blessed with water, forests and pastures: a perfect ecosystem which boasts many different species of animals. The Adamello Brenta Natural Park offers protection for the natural beauty of the valley. Its characteristic villages, full of history and traditions, are an expression of Alpine culture.

DAIRY
Malga Montagnoli is one of three dairies on the Spinale mountain, part of the “Regole di Spinale e Manez” common land which dates back to 1249. For centuries, the lives of the people from the villages of Ragoli, Montagne and Preore have been intertwined with the woods and pastures of Mount Spinale and the Val Manez: two areas which even today are still common land used by the people who are involved in the collective for the two mountains. The dairy comprises the cowshed and the cheesemaking hut, where the cowherds from the Polla family produce butter and cheeses every day. The young men look after 65 cows, taking them into the surrounding meadows and milking them twice a day, at 5 am and 5 pm.


PASTURE
Every day, our cows climb up the grassy slopes of the Spinale mountain to reach the prized grazing on the plateau. They are always on the move because cows are inquisitive and very active. They drink the pure water and feed on the flowers and fresh grass of the high pastures: their favourite meal is mountain thyme and “slavazöi”, a type of wild spinach. Thanks to this diet, their milk is flavoursome and nutritious, rich in beta carotene.


Cheese
It takes 45 litres of milk to produce a 9.5kg cheese. Only milk, rennet and salt, in cheeses of between 9 and 10 kg. The milk comes solely from Rendena breed cows fed exclusively on organic hay. Its taste reflects the flavours of the grass which the Rendena cows eat. Its aroma and flavour are also influenced by the ageing time: between 8 and 15 months at 12°C.
