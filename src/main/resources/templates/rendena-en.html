COWS
Rustic, independent and resilient, they have Slow Food status. The Rendena cow represents the history and tradition of this region. The first examples of the Rendena breed were introduced to the valley from Switzerland in 1700. With its dark coat and large, bright eyes, it is a dual-purpose cow (milk and meat). It is very well-suited to the summer pastures: trampling the soil and climbing steep slopes, it helps us to protect the land. The breed even has its own beauty contest, the “Giovenche di razza Rendena sfilata ... e dintorni” parade for Rendena heifers which is held in Pinzolo at the end of August to elect the best in show.


REGION
Sandwiched between the tonalite rock of the Adamello-Presanella ranges and the limestone of the Brenta Dolomites, the Val Rendena stretches from the hamlet of Verdesina up to Campo Carlo Magno, Madonna di Campiglio at a height of 1,650 m. It is blessed with water, forests and pastures: a perfect ecosystem which boasts many different species of animals. The Adamello Brenta Natural Park offers protection for the natural beauty of the valley. Its characteristic villages, full of history and traditions, are an expression of Alpine culture.

DAIRY
Malga Bandalors stands on the slopes of the Doss del Sabion above Giustino. In local dialect it is also known as the “Bregn de l’Ors”, the “bear’s drinking trough” because it appears that in the past bears would slake their thirst at the wooden trough. Between June and September, the cows graze the mountain pastures here. There is a cowshed and an old cheesemaker’s hut: the herdsman looks after 107 cows. The cows spend their whole day grazing on the pasture. At night, they return to the cowshed or, sometimes, they choose to sleep outside the building, beneath the stars.


PASTURE
Every day, our cows rtoam the slopes of the Doss del Sabion, going up towards Madonnina and then coming back down in the evening to the meadows around Malga Bandalors. They are always on the move because cows are inquisitive and very active. They drink the pure water and feed on the flowers and fresh grass of the high pastures: their favourite meal is mountain thyme and “slavazöi”, a type of wild spinach. Thanks to this diet, their milk is flavoursome and nutritious, rich in beta carotene.


CHEESE
It takes 45 litres of milk to produce a 4.5 kg cheese. Milk, rennet, salt.... and the craft of the cheesemaker: these are the secrets of a good Alpine dairy cheese. Its taste reflects the flavours of the grass which the Rendena cows eat. Its aroma and flavour are also influenced by the ageing time:
5 months at an average temperature of 10.5°C.
