COWS
Rustic, independent and resilient, they have Slow Food status. The Rendena cow represents the history and tradition of this region. The first examples of the Rendena breed were introduced to the valley from Switzerland in 1700. With its dark coat and large, bright eyes, it is a dual-purpose cow (milk and meat). It is very well-suited to the summer pastures: trampling the soil and climbing steep slopes, it helps us to protect the land. The breed even has its own beauty contest, the “Giovenche di razza Rendena sfilata ... e dintorni” parade for Rendena heifers which is held in Pinzolo at the end of August to elect the best in show.


REGION
Sandwiched between the tonalite rock of the Adamello-Presanella ranges and the limestone of the Brenta Dolomites, the Val Rendena stretches from the hamlet of Verdesina up to Campo Carlo Magno, Madonna di Campiglio at a height of 1,650 m. It is blessed with water, forests and pastures: a perfect ecosystem which boasts many different species of animals. The Adamello Brenta Natural Park offers protection for the natural beauty of the valley. Its characteristic villages, full of history and traditions, are an expression of Alpine culture.

DAIRY
Malga Ritort is one of the best known dairies in the valley thanks to its fortunate position: from here, there is a wonderful view of the Brenta Dolomites, the wonderful panorama revealing them in all their majesty. The large cowshed houses 70 Rendena breed cows and the farmhouse is also home to the Collini family (Luca and his wife and children) throughout the summer grazing season.


PASTURE
Every day, our cows graze on the flower-filled meadows around the dairy, heading towards the Canton di Ritort or Milegna areas. They are always on the move because cows are inquisitive and very active. They drink the pure water and feed on the flowers and fresh grass of the high pastures: their favourite meal is mountain thyme and “slavazöi”, a type of wild spinach. Thanks to this diet, their milk is flavoursome and nutritious, rich in beta carotene.


FORMAGGIO
The “Nostrano” cheese is produced using only milk from Rendena breed cattle fed on hay. The milk is the product of two consecutive milkings (the evening one is skimmed, the morning one is left whole). The starter used is a natural culture produced on the farm (whey starter). To produce a 7-8 kg cheese, around 100 litres of milk are required. Its taste reflects the flavours of the grass which the Rendena cows eat. Its aroma and flavour are also influenced by the ageing time: between 6 and 12 months at 8/10°C.
